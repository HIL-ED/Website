A New Japan:
A Meiji Restoration Story

You will not play a game. You will live a choice, again and again, and see where it leads.

In 1853, four foreign warships anchor off the coast of a country that has spent over two hundred years deliberately closed to the outside world. By 1889, that country has a written constitution, a national army built from conscripts instead of hereditary warriors, railways, telegraph lines, and factories — and a great many people who paid a steep price for all of it.

This is a story about that transformation, told from the ground up. You will choose who you are: a low-ranking samurai, a merchant's heir, a tenant farmer's child, or the daughter of a samurai household. You will make real decisions at real turning points, examine primary sources from the archive along the way, and pause periodically to reflect on what you're seeing. There is no score. There is no single correct path. There is only the question every person alive in Japan during these thirty-six years had to answer for themselves: when everything is changing, what do you hold onto, and what do you let go?

Whose eyes will you see this story through?

Each path lives through the same thirty-six years of history, but experiences them very differently.
