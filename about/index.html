We teach the classes we build games for.

Humanities Immersed Learning started in our own classrooms in Ohio. Like every teacher since the pandemic, we've faced the same wall: chronic absenteeism up, attention split a dozen ways, and students telling us "history isn't my thing" while loving the very stories, arguments, and art that history is made of.

Science classes have labs. Language arts has storytelling. Social studies too often gets one simulation a semester, squeezed in as a treat before the next unit. We thought that was backwards — the subject that is entirely about people making choices under pressure should be the most playable subject in school.

What We Believe

Everything we make is built on Experiential Narrative Education — our framework where teachers and students co-create a narrative that frames learning as an active, story-driven experience. Students engage with real academic content through roleplay, choice, challenge, and consequence. They get to try, fail safely, and reflect — and the content sticks because it's attached to something they lived, not something they highlighted.

Every game on this site started as an activity in a real classroom, run with real students, and refined by what actually worked. We measure the results the way teachers do: attendance, assessment scores, and whether students are still talking about the game weeks later.
